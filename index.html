A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Former President of India

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007.

Born - 15 October 1931 Rameswaram, India
Died - 27 July 2015 (aged 83) Shillong, India


Abdul Kalam, a visionary leader, renowned scientist, and beloved figure in Indian history. Dr. Kalam left an indelible mark on the nation and inspired countless individuals with his wisdom, humility, and unwavering commitment to progress. Join us as we explore the extraordinary journey of this extraordinary man.


Early Life and Education: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu, India. Despite humble beginnings, young Kalam was driven by an insatiable curiosity about the world around him. He pursued his education with great dedication, earning a degree in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later completing his aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

Scientific Achievements: Dr. Kalam's scientific contributions propelled him to national and international prominence. He played a pivotal role in the development of India's space and missile programs, serving as the project director for the indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and later leading the successful nuclear tests as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister. His pioneering work in these fields earned him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Vision for India: Beyond his scientific pursuits, Dr. Kalam possessed an unwavering vision for a prosperous and developed India. He firmly believed that science and technology could uplift the nation and its people. Throughout his life, he championed education, particularly in the fields of science and technology, as a means to empower the youth and drive India's progress. He passionately advocated for harnessing India's demographic dividend and encouraged entrepreneurship and innovation.

Inspiring Leadership and Presidency: In 2002, A.P.J. Abdul Kalam became the 11th President of India, a role he embraced wholeheartedly. During his presidency, he emphasized the importance of unity, particularly among the youth, to overcome challenges and achieve national goals. Dr. Kalam reached out to students and young minds across the country, delivering inspiring speeches and engaging in interactive sessions to motivate them to dream big and work tirelessly to achieve their aspirations.

Legacy and Inspiration: Dr. Kalam's humility, simplicity, and dedication to serving the nation have left an enduring impact on the hearts and minds of people across the globe. He believed in the potential of every individual and urged everyone to dream, innovate, and contribute to society. His books, including the popular "Wings of Fire," continue to inspire millions, providing insights into his life journey and teachings.
